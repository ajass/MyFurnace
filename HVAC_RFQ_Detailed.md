## Project Overview

We are seeking quotes for evaluation and potential replacement of our residential HVAC system. Based on diagnosis, our Goodman GSX14 14 SEER Central Air Conditioner has failed and requires replacement. Our Goodman furnace appears to be functional for heating purposes.

We are interested in exploring options for:
1. **AC-only replacement** - Replace just the failed air conditioning unit
2. **Full system upgrade** - Replace both furnace and AC unit to potentially benefit from rebates, tax credits, and improved efficiency

## Existing Equipment Specifications

Please reference the following documented equipment:
- **Furnace:** Goodman CAPF3137B6AA (see Furnace_details.md)
- **Air Conditioner:** Goodman GSX140301NC (14 SEER, R-410A refrigerant)

## Scope of Work

Contractors should provide quotes for both of the following scenarios:

### Option 1: AC Unit Replacement Only
- Removal and disposal of existing Goodman GSX140301NC AC unit
- Installation of new air conditioning unit compatible with existing furnace
- Necessary electrical, refrigerant line, and condensate drain connections
- System startup, testing, and customer orientation
- Permits and inspections as required by local jurisdiction

### Option 2: Complete System Replacement (Furnace + AC)
- Removal and disposal of existing Goodman CAPF3137B6AA furnace and GSX140301NC AC unit
- Installation of new matched furnace and air conditioning system
- Necessary electrical, gas line, refrigerant line, and condensate drain connections
- System startup, testing, and customer orientation
- Permits and inspections as required by local jurisdiction
- Ductwork evaluation and recommendations for improvements if needed

## Required Information in Quote

Please provide the following details for each option:

### 1. Equipment Specifications
- Manufacturer and model numbers for all proposed equipment
- SEER2/EER2 ratings for cooling equipment
- AFUE rating for heating equipment
- BTU/h capacity for both heating and cooling
- Refrigerant type (prefer R-454B or R-32 for newer systems)
- Warranty details (parts, labor, compressor/heat exchanger)
- Estimated equipment lifespan

### 2. Pricing Breakdown
- Equipment costs (itemized)
- Labor costs (itemized)
- Permit and inspection fees
- Disposal/recycling fees for old equipment
- Any additional materials or supplies
- **Total project cost** for each option
- Payment schedule and terms

### 3. Rebates and Incentives
- Manufacturer/factory rebates available
- State of Oregon energy efficiency rebates
- Federal tax credits (Inflation Reduction Act - 25C and 25D)
- Local utility rebates (if applicable)
- Assistance with rebate application process
- Estimated net cost after all applicable incentives

### 4. Project Timeline
- Estimated start date upon acceptance
- Duration of installation work
- Any dependencies or factors that could affect timeline

### 5. Company Information
- Contractor license numbers and certifications
- Years in business and local references
- Insurance coverage (liability and workers' compensation)
- Warranty on installation work
- Service and maintenance offerings post-installation
- Emergency service availability

### 6. Efficiency and Performance Information
- Estimated annual energy savings compared to existing system
- Sound ratings for outdoor units
- Smart thermostat compatibility and recommendations
- Indoor air quality options (filtration, ventilation, humidity control)

## Evaluation Criteria

Quotes will be evaluated based on:
1. Total cost after rebates and incentives
2. Equipment efficiency and performance specifications
3. Warranty coverage (equipment and labor)
4. Contractor reputation and references
5. Completeness and clarity of proposal
6. Timeline and project management approach
